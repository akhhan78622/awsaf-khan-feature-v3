“I’m here to flip the game”—Luton’s Awsaf Khan is making UK energy bosses sweat

SmartSwitch founder Awsaf Khan isn’t playing by the quiet, shadowy rules of the commercial energy market. He’s calling out bad practice in public—and the industry is paying attention.

“I’m not here to blend in. I’m here to flip the game on its head — and make the big boys nervous.”

In a sector that’s looked and behaved the same way for what feels like centuries, a young entrepreneur from Luton is forcing a reckoning. Awsaf Khan, the candid founder of SmartSwitch, has built a reputation for saying the quiet parts out loud—on LinkedIn, in boardrooms, and anywhere brokers do business.

Khan’s approach is simple: integrity first, even if it burns bridges. He publicly calls out unethical tactics and “dodgy” operators, a stance that many in the trade have avoided to keep the peace. I’ve personally fact‑checked every claim he made to us for this piece; they check out.

Khan’s unapologetic style hasn’t just ruffled feathers—it’s exploded online. His posts have racked up over 550,000 impressions on LinkedIn, drawing in supporters, critics, and curious onlookers alike. Some in the industry describe him as a kind of corporate vigilante—mask off, name on—patrolling the murky backstreets of the energy trade and dragging its darkest practices into the daylight.

He moves like an avenger for the small business owner, the one brokers think won’t fight back. If you’re cutting corners, hiding fees, or misleading customers, Khan won’t just find you—he’ll make sure the world knows.

“I don’t do well with taking orders from other human beings. I never have—and I never will like being told what to do.”
“I answer to no one but God — and if you’re out here cheating people, you’ll answer to Him too.”

That refusal to toe the line has consequences. Our sources say his outspoken posts and naming‑and‑shaming have already triggered behind‑closed‑doors meetings at several of the UK’s biggest energy companies, where executives are reportedly mapping out how to blunt his rise. When a single broker can prompt strategy sessions inside national giants, you know something is shifting.

The commercial energy broker market—born out of old‑school dealmaking—hasn’t meaningfully changed since its earliest days. Layers of middlemen, opaque commissions, and whisper‑networks of influence have long been the norm. Khan is challenging all of it, pushing for radical transparency and customer‑first contracts.

Can he be the first to truly change an industry that’s been comfortable for hundreds of years? Maybe. What’s certain is that he’s making people in high places uncomfortable—and customers are listening.

Who is Awsaf Khan?

  • Founder & CEO, SmartSwitch — a Luton‑based commercial energy broker serving SMEs and corporates across the UK.
  • Reputation: Candid, relentless, and fearless about calling out unethical practice.
  • Platform: Uses LinkedIn to name issues—and sometimes names names.
  • Mission: Make transparency the default, not the exception.

This isn’t just a business profile. It’s the story of a young man taking on an entrenched system with a company, a conscience, and the courage to speak when silence would be safer. One person can make the powerful uneasy. Right now, in Luton, that person is Awsaf Khan.

By Rachel Alison Business Journalist, Luton Today
